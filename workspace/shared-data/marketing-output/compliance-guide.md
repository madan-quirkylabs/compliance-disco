# DPDP Act 2023 — Compliance Guide for Businesses

**Your practical roadmap to the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 and the DPDP Rules, 2025**

---

## 1. Overview

The Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDP Act) is India's landmark data protection legislation. Together with the DPDP Rules, 2025 (notified on 13 November 2025), it establishes a comprehensive framework for processing digital personal data.

**Key fact:** Most substantive rules come into force on **13 May 2027** — 18 months from publication — giving businesses time to prepare.

---

## 2. Who Does This Apply To?

| Role | Definition | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Data Fiduciary** | Anyone who determines the purpose and means of processing personal data | Your company |
| **Data Processor** | Anyone who processes data on behalf of a Data Fiduciary | Cloud provider, payroll vendor |
| **Data Principal** | The individual whose data is processed | Your customers, employees |
| **Significant Data Fiduciary (SDF)** | Notified Data Fiduciaries with additional obligations | Large platforms, high-risk processors |

**Applies if:**
- You collect digital personal data within India
- You process data outside India in connection with offering goods/services to individuals in India

---

## 3. Core Obligations at a Glance

### 3.1 Lawful Processing
You may process personal data only:
- With **consent** (free, specific, informed, unconditional, unambiguous, affirmative action), OR
- For specified **legitimate uses** (voluntary provision, state benefits, legal obligations, medical emergencies, employment, etc.)

### 3.2 Notice Requirements
Every consent request must be accompanied by a notice containing:
- Itemised personal data being collected
- Specific purpose of processing
- How to exercise rights and withdraw consent
- How to complain to the Data Protection Board
- Available in English or any Eighth Schedule language (upon request)

### 3.3 Consent Management
- Consent must be as easy to **withdraw** as it was to give
- Upon withdrawal: **cease processing** within a reasonable time
- Data Principal may use a **Consent Manager** (registered with the Board)
- **Burden of proof** is on the Data Fiduciary

### 3.4 Data Protection Measures
| Requirement | Details |
|------------|---------|
| **Security safeguards** | Encryption, access controls, logging/monitoring, backups (Rule 6) |
| **Data Processor contracts** | Must have a valid written contract |
| **Accuracy** | Data used for decisions/disclosure must be complete, accurate, consistent |
| **Log retention** | Minimum 1 year |

### 3.5 Breach Notification
| Timeline | What to do |
|----------|-----------|
| **Without delay** | Intimate the Board (initial notification) + each affected Data Principal |
| **Within 72 hours** | Submit detailed report to the Board |

### 3.6 Erasure Obligations
- **On consent withdrawal:** Erase unless retention required by law
- **Purpose no longer served:** Erase when reasonable to assume purpose is no longer served
- **Inactive accounts** (e-commerce, gaming, social media): Erase after 3 years of inactivity (with 48-hour prior notice)
- **Minimum retention:** Logs and personal data for at least 1 year

### 3.7 Grievance Redressal
- Establish an effective mechanism
- **Respond within 90 days** of receiving a grievance
- Publish contact information of your Data Protection Officer or authorised person

## 4. Obligations for Significant Data Fiduciaries (SDFs)

If notified as an SDF, you must additionally:

| Obligation | Frequency |
|-----------|-----------|
| Appoint a **Data Protection Officer** (based in India) | Ongoing |
| Appoint an **independent data auditor** | Ongoing |
| **Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA)** | Every 12 months |
| **Periodic audit** | Every 12 months |
| Report DPIA/audit results to the Board | As per Rule 13 |
| Algorithmic software due diligence | As per Rule 13 |
| Data localisation for specified data | As notified |

---

## 5. Children's Data — Extra Care Required

- Obtain **verifiable parental consent** before processing a child's data
- **No** tracking, behavioural monitoring, or targeted advertising directed at children
- **No** processing likely to cause detrimental effect on a child's well-being
- Exemptions apply for certain classes of Data Fiduciaries (Fourth Schedule)

---

## 6. Rights of Data Principals

| Right | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Right to information** | Know what data is collected and why |
| **Right to correction** | Correct inaccurate or misleading data |
| **Right to erasure** | Request deletion of personal data |
| **Right to grievance redressal** | Have complaints addressed within 90 days |
| **Right to nominate** | Nominate a person to exercise rights after death/incapacity |

### Duties of Data Principals
- Comply with applicable laws
- Not impersonate or suppress material information
- Furnish verifiably authentic information
- Not register false grievances

---

## 7. Data Transfers Outside India

- The Central Government may notify **restricted territories**
- Data Fiduciaries must comply with such restrictions
- SDFs may have additional localisation requirements for specified data

---

## 8. Penalties You Need to Know

| Violation | Maximum Penalty |
|-----------|----------------|
| Failure to implement security safeguards | **₹250 crore** |
| Breach notification failures | **₹200 crore** |
| Children's data obligations violations | **₹200 crore** |
| SDF obligations violations | **₹150 crore** |
| General non-compliance | **₹50 crore** |
| Data Principal duty violations | **₹10,000** |

**Penalty determination factors:** Nature/gravity/duration of breach, type of data, repetitive nature, gain/loss from breach, mitigation efforts, proportionality, impact on affected persons.

## 9. Implementation Timeline

| Date | Milestone |
|------|-----------|
| **11 Aug 2023** | DPDP Act receives President's assent |
| **13 Nov 2025** | Rules 1, 2, 17-21 come into force (definitions, appointments, digital office) |
| **13 Nov 2026** | Rule 4 in force (Consent Manager registration) |
| **13 May 2027** | **Major rules come into force** — notice, security, breach notification, verifiable consent, erasure, grievance redressal, transfers |
| *To be notified* | Act provisions commencement; SDF notifications |

## 10. Quick Action Checklist for Your Team

- [ ] **Identify** what personal data you collect and process
- [ ] **Map** data flows — collection, storage, sharing, retention
- [ ] **Review** consent mechanisms — are they free, specific, informed, unambiguous?
- [ ] **Update** privacy notices — itemised data + purpose, withdrawal link, complaint channel
- [ ] **Implement** security safeguards — encryption, access controls, logging
- [ ] **Draft** breach notification procedure — internal escalation, Board notification, Data Principal notification
- [ ] **Establish** grievance redressal mechanism — 90-day response capability
- [ ] **Audit** Data Processor contracts — ensure valid written agreements
- [ ] **Prepare** for children's data — verifiable parental consent flow
- [ ] **Assess** if you qualify as an SDF — prepare for DPIA, audit, DPO appointment
- [ ] **Set up** data erasure workflows — consent withdrawal, inactivity periods
- [ ] **Train** your team on DPDP Act obligations and rights
