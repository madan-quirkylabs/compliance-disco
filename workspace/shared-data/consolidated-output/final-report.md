# DPDP Act 2023 Compliance Report

## Executive Summary

This report covers 10 obligations under DPDP Act 2023, issued by DPDP Board.

## Obligations
- Consent Requirement: No personal data processing without consent. (Section 4(1))
- Purpose Limitation: Data shall not be processed for purposes other than consent. (Section 5(1))
- Notice Requirements: Must provide notice before collecting consent. (Section 6)
- Children's Data: Verifiable parental consent required. (Section 8)
- Breach Notification: Must notify Board and individuals of breaches. (Section 10)
- SDF Obligations: DPO, audits, DPIA required. (Section 11)
- Cross-Border Transfer: Transfer allowed except to restricted countries. (Section 16)
- Data Principal Rights: Access, correction, erasure, grievance, nomination. (Section 12)
- Data Retention: Erase when purpose fulfilled or consent withdrawn. (Section 14)
- Penalties: Up to Rs. 250 crore. (Section 33)

## Penalties
- Security safeguards failure: Up to Rs. 250 crore
- Breach notification failure: Up to Rs. 200 crore
