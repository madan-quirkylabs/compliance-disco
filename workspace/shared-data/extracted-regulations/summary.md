# Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDP Act) and Digital Personal Data Protection Rules, 2025

## Issuing Body
- DPDP Board (Data Protection Board of India), as established under the Act. The Rules are issued by the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology, Government of India.

## Publication Dates
- Act: 11th August 2023 (assent of President).
- Rules: 13th November 2025 (notification in the Gazette).

## Applicability
- The Act applies to processing of digital personal data within India where data is collected in digital form or digitised subsequently; also applies to processing outside India if in connection with offering goods/services to Data Principals in India.
- Exemptions: personal data processed for personal/domestic purposes, publicly available data by Data Principal or under legal obligation, certain processing by State instrumentalities (notified), research/archiving/statistical purposes (subject to standards), and other specified exemptions.
- The Rules specify classes of Data Fiduciaries and purposes for which certain obligations are exempted (e.g., for child data processing).

## Overview of Purpose
To provide for processing of digital personal data in a manner that recognises both the right of individuals to protect their personal data and the need to process such personal data for lawful purposes.

## Section-by-Section Breakdown
### Act (Key Provisions)
- **Chapter I (Preliminary):** Definitions and application (sections 1-3).
- **Chapter II (Obligations of Data Fiduciary):** Grounds for processing (consent or legitimate uses), notice requirements, consent provisions, general obligations (security safeguards, breach notification, erasure, grievance redressal), additional obligations for processing children's data, and obligations for Significant Data Fiduciaries (DPIA, audit, Data Protection Officer).
- **Chapter III (Rights and Duties of Data Principal):** Right to access information, correction and erasure, grievance redressal, right to nominate, duties of Data Principal.
- **Chapter IV (Special Provisions):** Restrictions on transfer of personal data outside India; exemptions from certain provisions.
- **Chapter V (Data Protection Board of India):** Establishment, composition, qualifications, disqualifications, resignation, proceedings.
- **Chapter VI (Powers, Functions and Procedure of Board):** Powers to inquire into breaches, impose penalties, issue directions, conduct digital proceedings.
- **Chapter VII (Appeal and Alternate Dispute Resolution):** Appeal to Appellate Tribunal, mediation, voluntary undertakings.
- **Chapter VIII (Penalties and Adjudication):** Penalties as per Schedule; factors for determining amount.
- **Chapter IX (Miscellaneous):** Protection of action taken in good faith, power to call for information, power of Central Government to issue directions, consistency with other laws, bar of jurisdiction, rule-making power.

### Rules (Key Provisions)
- **Rule 1:** Short title and commencement (different dates for different rules).
- **Rule 2:** Definitions (including "techno-legal measures", "user account", "verifiable consent").
- **Rule 3:** Notice requirements (clear and plain language, itemised personal data and purpose, communication link for withdrawal, rights, complaints).
- **Rule 4:** Registration and obligations of Consent Manager (conditions in First Schedule).
- **Rule 5:** Processing by State and instrumentalities for subsidies, benefits, etc. (standards in Second Schedule).
- **Rule 6:** Reasonable security safeguards (encryption, access controls, logs, backups, contracts with Data Processors, retention of logs for one year).
- **Rule 7:** Intimation of personal data breach to affected Data Principals and Board (description, consequences, measures, contact; to Board within 72 hours detailed report).
- **Rule 8:** Time period for specified purpose to be deemed no longer served; erasure obligations; retention of logs for at least one year.
- **Rule 9:** Publishing contact information of Data Protection Officer or person answering questions.
- **Rule 10:** Verifiable consent for child data (due diligence for parent's identity and age).
- **Rule 11:** Verifiable consent for person with disability (guardian verification).
- **Rule 12:** Exemptions from child data obligations (classes of Data Fiduciaries and purposes in Fourth Schedule).
- **Rule 13:** Additional obligations of Significant Data Fiduciary (annual DPIA and audit, report to Board, algorithmic software diligence, data localisation restrictions for specified personal data).
- **Rule 14:** Rights of Data Principals (publishing means for requests, grievance redressal within 90 days, nomination of individuals).
- **Rule 15:** Transfer of personal data outside India subject to restrictions specified by Central Government.
- **Rule 16:** Exemption from Act for research, archiving or statistical purposes (standards in Second Schedule).
- **Rules 17-21:** Chairperson and Members appointment, salary, meetings, digital office functioning, terms of officers.
- **Rule 22:** Appeal to Appellate Tribunal (digital form, fee).
- **Rule 23:** Calling for information from Data Fiduciary or intermediary.

### Schedules
- **First Schedule:** Conditions for registration and obligations of Consent Manager.
- **Second Schedule:** Standards for processing by State and for research/archiving.
- **Third Schedule:** Time periods for erasure for specified classes of Data Fiduciaries (e-commerce, online gaming, social media).
- **Fourth Schedule:** Classes of Data Fiduciaries and purposes exempt from child data obligations.
- **Fifth Schedule:** Terms of service for Chairperson and Members.
- **Sixth Schedule:** Terms for officers and employees of Board.
- **Seventh Schedule:** Purposes and authorised persons for calling information.
