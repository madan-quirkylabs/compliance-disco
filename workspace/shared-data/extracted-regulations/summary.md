# Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 and Rules, 2025

**Regulation Name:** Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDP Act) and Digital Personal Data Protection Rules, 2025

**Issuing Body:** Parliament of India (Act), Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (Rules)

**Publication Dates:**
- DPDP Act: 11 August 2023 (assent), published in Gazette on 12 August 2023
- DPDP Rules: 13 November 2025 (notification)

**Applicability:**
- Applies to processing of digital personal data within India (whether digitized after collection or originally digital)
- Also applies to processing outside India if related to offering goods or services to Data Principals in India
- Exemptions: personal processing, publicly available data by Data Principal or under legal obligation
- Specific rules have staggered commencement dates

**Purpose:** To provide for processing of digital personal data in a manner that recognizes both the right of individuals to protect their personal data and the need to process such personal data for lawful purposes.

## Section-by-Section Breakdown

### DPDP Act 2023
- **Chapter I (Preliminary):** Short title, commencement, definitions (sections 1-2)
- **Chapter II (Obligations of Data Fiduciary):** 
  - Section 4: Grounds for processing – consent or certain legitimate uses
  - Section 5: Notice requirements before/at time of consent
  - Section 6: Consent requirements (free, specific, informed, unconditional, unambiguous)
  - Section 7: Certain legitimate uses (e.g., State functions, medical emergencies, employment)
  - Section 8: General obligations (data processing contract, accuracy, security safeguards, breach notification, erasure, grievance redressal)
  - Section 9: Processing of children’s data (verifiable parental consent, prohibition on detrimental processing, tracking, targeted advertising)
  - Section 10: Additional obligations for Significant Data Fiduciaries (appoint DPO, data auditor, DPIA, audit)
- **Chapter III (Rights and Duties of Data Principal):** 
  - Section 11: Right to access information about personal data
  - Section 12: Right to correction and erasure
  - Section 13: Right to grievance redressal
  - Section 14: Right to nominate
  - Section 15: Duties (comply with laws, not impersonate, not suppress, not frivolous complaints, furnish authentic info)
- **Chapter IV (Special Provisions):** 
  - Section 16: Restriction on cross-border transfer
  - Section 17: Exemptions (legal rights, judicial functions, prevention/detection of offences, etc.)
- **Chapter V (Data Protection Board of India):** Establishment, composition, qualifications, salary, term (2 years), disqualifications, resignation, proceedings
- **Chapter VI (Powers, Functions, Procedure of Board):** 
  - Section 27: Powers – inquire into breaches, impose penalties
  - Section 28: Procedure – digital office, inquiry, interim orders
- **Chapter VII (Appeal and Alternate Dispute Resolution):** 
  - Section 29: Appeal to Appellate Tribunal (TDSAT), 60 days
  - Section 30: Orders executable as decree
  - Section 31: Mediation
  - Section 32: Voluntary undertaking
- **Chapter VIII (Penalties and Adjudication):** 
  - Section 33: Penalties as per Schedule
  - Section 34: Sums credited to Consolidated Fund
- **Chapter IX (Miscellaneous):** Good faith protection, power to call for information, Central Government directions, consistency, bar of jurisdiction, rule-making, laying, amendments, power to remove difficulties
- **Schedule:** Penalties from ₹10,000 to ₹250 crore

### DPDP Rules 2025
- **Rule 1:** Short title and commencement (staggered: Rules 1,2,17-21 effective on publication; Rule 4 after 1 year; remaining after 18 months)
- **Rule 2:** Definitions (techno-legal measures, user account, verifiable consent)
- **Rule 3:** Notice given by Data Fiduciary (independent, clear language, itemized description, purpose, withdrawal/rights/complaint information)
- **Rule 4:** Registration and obligations of Consent Manager (conditions, obligations, suspension/cancellation)
- **Rule 5:** Processing by State for subsidies etc. (follow Second Schedule)
- **Rule 6:** Reasonable security safeguards (encryption, access control, logs, backups, contracts, organizational measures)
- **Rule 7:** Intimation of personal data breach (to affected Data Principal: description, consequences, measures; to Board: immediate description + detailed report within 72 hours)
- **Rule 8:** Time period for specified purpose deemed no longer served (Third Schedule classes; minimum 1 year retention of logs; erasure after period) 
- **Rule 9:** Contact information of person to answer questions (publish on website/app)
- **Rule 10:** Verifiable consent for child (due diligence via identity/age details or virtual token from authorized entity)
- **Rule 11:** Verifiable consent for person with disability (verification of lawful guardian appointment)
- **Rule 12:** Exemptions from child-related obligations (Fourth Schedule classes and purposes)
- **Rule 13:** Additional obligations of Significant Data Fiduciary (annual DPIA and audit, report to Board, algorithmic risk assessment, restriction on transfer of specified personal data)
- **Rule 14:** Rights of Data Principals (means to exercise, grievance redressal within 90 days, nomination)
- **Rule 15:** Transfer of personal data outside India (subject to Central Government restrictions)
- **Rule 16:** Exemption for research, archiving, statistical purposes (follow Second Schedule)
- **Rule 17:** Appointment of Chairperson and Members (Search-cum-Selection Committee)
- **Rule 18:** Salary, allowances (Fifth Schedule)
- **Rule 19:** Procedure for meetings of Board (quorum 1/3, voting, emergent powers)
- **Rule 20:** Board as digital office
- **Rule 21:** Terms of officers and employees (Sixth Schedule)
- **Rule 22:** Appeal to Appellate Tribunal (digital form, fee)
- **Rule 23:** Calling for information from Data Fiduciary or intermediary (Seventh Schedule purposes)
- **First Schedule:** Conditions and obligations for Consent Managers (net worth ₹2 crore, etc.)
- **Second Schedule:** Standards for State processing and research (lawful, limited, accurate, secure, etc.)
- **Third Schedule:** Time periods for erasure (e-commerce ≥2 crore users: 3 years; online gaming ≥50 lakh users: 3 years; social media ≥2 crore users: 3 years)
- **Fourth Schedule:** Exemptions from child provisions (classes: healthcare, education, etc.; purposes: legal functions, subsidies, email accounts, child safety)
- **Fifth Schedule:** Salary and terms of Chairperson/Members (salary ₹4.5 lakh/month for Chairperson, ₹4 lakh/month for Members)
- **Sixth Schedule:** Terms of Board officers/employees (deputation up to 5 years, gratuity, etc.)
- **Seventh Schedule:** Purposes for calling information (State sovereignty, legal functions, assessment for Significant Data Fiduciary)

**Key Obligations Overview:** Data Fiduciaries must obtain valid consent, provide notices, implement security safeguards, report breaches, erase data when purpose is served, establish grievance mechanisms. Significant Data Fiduciaries have additional requirements. Consent Managers must be registered and follow strict operational rules. Specific rules for children and persons with disabilities.

**Penalties:** Up to ₹250 crore for breach of security safeguards, ₹200 crore for breach notification failures or child-related violations, ₹150 crore for Significant Data Fiduciary obligations, ₹10,000 for Data Principal duty breaches, ₹50 crore for other violations.
